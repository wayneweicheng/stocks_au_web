## Predictive Logic (Hierarchical Importance)

The following rules are QQQ-specific and were selected for out-of-sample usefulness, interpretability, and consistency with market structure.

### 1. Tier 1: Primary Alpha And Risk Triggers

#### Negative_GEX_AND_High_VIX (Capitulation Reversion)
- **Signal:** Bullish mean-reversion bias, usually strongest for tactical buy-the-dip setups.
- **Condition:** `Negative_GEX_AND_High_VIX = 1`, equivalent to negative GEX with elevated VIX stress.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train n=52, avg TomorrowChange +0.294%, win rate 63.5%; validation n=10, avg +0.875%, win rate 80.0%; test n=37, avg +0.084%, win rate 54.1%. The test edge weakened, but the 5-day follow-through stayed positive at +0.525%.
- **Rationale:** QQQ tends to rebound after negative-gamma volatility stress because dealer hedging can become exhausted and then reverse mechanically.
- **Priority:** Bullish unless option flow shows a near-term call wall directly overhead and fresh put OI is expanding aggressively at or above spot.

#### GEX_Turned_Negative (Gamma Flip Risk)
- **Signal:** Mildly bearish to bearish for tomorrow; volatility-expansion warning.
- **Condition:** `GEX_Turned_Negative = 1`.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train n=51, avg TomorrowChange -0.233%; validation n=11, avg -0.109%; test n=9, avg -0.267%, test win rate only 44.4%.
- **Rationale:** A transition from positive to negative gamma shifts QQQ from a dampened regime into a more reflexive regime where selloffs can extend.
- **Priority:** If this fires without a stronger capitulation-reversal setup, downgrade bullish forecasts and prefer sell-rip or defensive positioning.

#### RSI < 30 (Oversold Bounce)
- **Signal:** Bullish bounce.
- **Condition:** `RSI < 30`.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train n=37, avg TomorrowChange +0.575%, win rate 64.9%; test n=4, avg +1.485%, win rate 75.0%. Validation had no occurrences, so treat the sample as powerful but sparse.
- **Rationale:** Deep oversold conditions in QQQ are often short-lived, especially when option put walls sit below spot and can force dealer support.
- **Priority:** Strong bullish if not contradicted by a fresh `GEX_Turned_Negative` event and heavy same-day put buying at near-the-money strikes.

### 2. Tier 2: Regime And Continuation Signals

#### Golden_Setup (Trend Continuation)
- **Signal:** Bullish continuation, especially over 2-5 trading days.
- **Condition:** `Golden_Setup = 1`.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train n=37, avg TomorrowChange +0.228%, Next5DaysChange +1.750%; test n=10, avg TomorrowChange +0.465%, win rate 70.0%, Next5DaysChange +2.340%. Validation had no occurrences.
- **Rationale:** This setup captures aligned trend/momentum structure. In QQQ, when it appears, pullbacks are more likely to be bought.
- **Priority:** If active, it overrides ordinary overbought warnings from RSI unless option walls show extreme overhead resistance within roughly 0.5-1.0% of spot.

#### GEX Positive And GEXChange Positive (Stabilizing Gamma Rebuild)
- **Signal:** Mildly bullish tomorrow; constructive support regime.
- **Condition:** `GEX > 0 AND GEXChange > 0`.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train n=88, avg TomorrowChange +0.037%; validation n=33, avg +0.104%; test n=22, avg +0.326%, win rate 68.2%, Next5DaysChange +0.972%.
- **Rationale:** Positive and rising GEX tends to suppress volatility and rewards dip-buying. It is more useful as a stabilizer than as a standalone explosive-upside trigger.
- **Priority:** Supports bullish or neutral-bullish forecasts when no Tier 1 bearish risk is active.

#### DarkPool Ratio > 2.0 (Institutional Accumulation)
- **Signal:** Mildly bullish support floor.
- **Condition:** `Stock_DarkPoolBuySellRatio > 2.0`.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train n=88, avg TomorrowChange +0.116%; validation n=20, avg +0.273%; test n=20, avg +0.228%, Next5DaysChange +1.690%.
- **Rationale:** Strong dark-pool buy skew often indicates institutional demand beneath the tape.
- **Priority:** Use as confirmation, not a standalone signal. If ratio is only 1.5-2.0, treat as supportive but not decisive.

### 3. Tier 3: Context And Conflict Resolution

#### RSI > 70 (Momentum, Not Automatic Bearishness)
- **Signal:** Caution only; do not short solely because QQQ is overbought.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train n=126, avg TomorrowChange +0.028%; validation n=51, avg +0.033%; test n=25, avg +0.287%, win rate 56.0%.
- **Rationale:** QQQ can stay overbought during strong technology momentum. Overbought readings matter more when paired with a fresh gamma flip negative, a strong overhead call wall, or deteriorating dark-pool flow.
- **Priority:** Downgrade from strongly bullish to mildly bullish/neutral if RSI > 70 and no Tier 1 or Tier 2 bullish trigger is active.

#### Price Above SMA20 And SMA50
- **Signal:** Trend-support context, not alpha by itself.
- **Historical Evidence:** Test split avg TomorrowChange +0.073%, win rate 53.0%.
- **Rationale:** Above-SMA structure confirms trend health, but it is too broad to drive a forecast alone.
- **Priority:** Use it to avoid overly bearish calls when other evidence is mixed.

#### Potential Swing Up With Negative GEX Change
- **Signal:** Weak/legacy bullish context only.
- **Historical Evidence:** Train and validation were positive, but test n=17 had avg TomorrowChange -0.499% and win rate 29.4%.
- **Rationale:** The relationship appears regime-dependent and has degraded recently.
- **Priority:** Do not use this as a bullish trigger unless confirmed by GEX rebuild, dark-pool accumulation, and supportive option walls.

## Option Flow Interpretation

Use the option datasets as current-context overlays. They should refine, not replace, the validated daily-feature rules.

### Latest Option Trades

Analyze the latest option trades section for:

- Total call size and estimated premium versus total put size and estimated premium.
- Large repeated trades at the same strike/expiry.
- Late-day clustering, especially trades after 3:00 PM New York time.
- Whether trades are near spot, in-the-money, or far out-of-the-money.
- Whether call flow confirms a bullish daily-feature signal or put flow contradicts it.

Heavy put trades near spot should downgrade bullish forecasts. Heavy call trades near spot should confirm bullish continuation only when GEX is stable or rising.

### Option OI Changes (Yesterday Versus Today)

Analyze the option OI changes section as newly built positioning:

- Near-term 0-7 DTE OI changes have the highest weight.
- Large positive put OI changes below spot can create support, but large positive put OI changes at or above spot can signal defensive pressure.
- Large positive call OI changes above spot can become resistance unless price is already breaking through with strong momentum.
- Compare total positive call OI change versus total positive put OI change.

If fresh put OI additions dominate fresh call OI additions by more than 2:1, treat the tape as defensive unless daily features show a clear capitulation-reversal setup.

### Top Options By Current Open Interest

Analyze the top options by current open interest section for gamma walls:

- **Primary put wall:** Highest near-term put OI below or near spot.
- **Primary call wall:** Highest near-term call OI above or near spot.
- Prioritize 0-7 DTE, then 8-14 DTE, then 15-30 DTE, then 30-90 DTE.
- A put wall far below spot is crash protection, not an immediate buy level.
- A call wall directly above spot can cap upside and turn a bullish forecast into mildly bullish/neutral.

For trade levels, anchor buy-dip and sell-rip ranges to specific strikes from the OI data whenever available.

### 30-Minute Price Bars

Analyze the 30-minute price bars section for:

- VWAP clusters near option walls.
- Intraday support/resistance from repeated lows/highs.
- Whether price is holding above VWAP into the close.
- Whether the final trading sessions show accumulation, distribution, or failed breakout behavior.

Use 30-minute bars as timing confirmation. They should not override Tier 1 daily signals unless the intraday structure is clearly invalidating the setup.
